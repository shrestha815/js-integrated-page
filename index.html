The Great Gatsby

Author: F. Scott Fitzgerald

Rating: 4.5

The Great Gatsby is a 1925 novel by American writer F. Scott Fitzgerald. Set in the Jazz Age on Long Island, the novel depicts first-person narrator Nick Carraway's interactions with mysterious millionaire Jay Gatsby and Gatsby's obsession with the beautiful former debutante Daisy Buchanan.

To Kill a Mockingbird

Author: Harper Lee

Rating: 4.8

To Kill a Mockingbird is a novel by Harper Lee published in 1960. It was immediately successful, winning the Pulitzer Prize, and has become a classic of modern American literature. The plot and characters are loosely based on the author's observations of her family, her neighbors, and an event that occurred near her hometown of Monroeville, Alabama, in 1936, when she was 10 years old.

Top Rated

1984

Author: George Orwell

Rating: 4.7

1984 is a dystopian social science fiction novel and cautionary tale, written by the English writer George Orwell. It was published in 1949 and is set in a totalitarian society ruled by the Party and its leader, Big Brother.

About Good Reads

Good Reads is a platform that allows users to discover and share their favorite books. It provides a space for readers to connect, review, and recommend books to others.

Users can create virtual bookshelves, track their reading progress, and participate in discussions about their favorite titles. The platform also offers personalized recommendations based on users' reading habits and preferences.

With a vast library of books across various genres, Good Reads is a haven for book lovers. Whether you're looking for the latest best sellers or hidden gems, Good Reads has something for everyone.
